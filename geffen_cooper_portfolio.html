Résumé Scholar GitHub LinkedIn Email

I'm a fourth-year Ph.D. student in the System Level Design group at UT Austin, advised by Professor Radu Marculescu. Before my Ph.D., I graduated from UC Santa Barbara in 2022 with a B.S. in Computer Engineering.

I build machine learning systems for batteryless, energy-harvesting sensors — devices that run entirely on ambient power and transmit data only when they can. The core challenge: how do you do reliable, real-time inference when your sensor goes dark unpredictably and every packet costs energy?

My work spans the full stack, from hardware-level energy harvesting circuits to the algorithms that run on top of them. I've developed learned sampling strategies for gesture recognition under energy constraints, RL-based policies for deciding when a batteryless device should spend energy transmitting, and general sensor selection methods for practical deep learning-based HAR.

Research

⚡

Batteryless & Energy-Harvesting Systems

Designing ML pipelines for sensors powered entirely by ambient energy — solar, kinetic, RF — that operate intermittently and asynchronously.

🏃

Human Activity Recognition

Learning to classify motion and gestures from sparse, irregular data streams where power interruptions are the norm, not the exception.

📡

Edge AI & Embedded ML

Developing low-power deep learning algorithms for resource-constrained microcontrollers and wearable IoT devices.

📊

Time-Series & Sensor Fusion

Efficient algorithms for processing asynchronous multi-sensor data, including learned sampling strategies and sensor selection methods.
